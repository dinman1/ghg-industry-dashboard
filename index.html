Which Industries Drive Climate Change?

Created by Daunte Inman

For this project, I am looking at greenhouse gas emissions across industries in the United States. Instead of only focusing on total pollution, I wanted to ask a different question: how much greenhouse gas is produced per dollar of economic activity?

Data source: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, Supply Chain Greenhouse Gas Emission Factors v1.3 by NAICS-6.

Key takeaway: What I noticed is that emission intensity is not spread evenly. A small number of industries produce much more greenhouse gas per dollar than most other industries, which shows that climate action may be more effective when it focuses on the highest-emission areas.
